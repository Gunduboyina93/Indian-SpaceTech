PARLIAMENT GIVES BAS-1 MISSION UPDATE

Confirms ISRO has finalised configuration of all BAS modules

Published on December 13, 2025 | Category: Space

A press release dated 3rd December, 2025 revealed that ISRO has finalised the configuration of all five modules of India's first space station - Bharatiya Antariksh Station. This came after a National Level Review Committee approved its design earlier. ISRO also revealed that "overall system engineering of BAS-01 module and technology development activities of various subsystems were progressing well".

Development and launch of the first module BAS-1 is still targeted for 2028, with the full station configuration (five modules) slated to be completed by 2035. ISRO also has plans to co-operate with international space agencies in operating BAS. To this end, ISRO is incoporating several international standards in the design of its BAS modules and their subsystems.

ISRO also hopes that such collaborations will help it in the development of technologies for Indian Human Space programme - The GAGANYAAN Mission.

The BAS program is envisaged as the logical successor to the Gaganyaan program, whose first crewed mission in 2027 will put an Indian in space, launched from Indian soil - a first. These programms, in turn, are part of the larger 'India Space Vision 2047', with the aim of landing the first Indian on the Moon by 2040.
